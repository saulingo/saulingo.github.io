Spanish Naming

More complete information can be found in Wikipedia - Spanish naming customs.

People in Spain usually have a given name (nombre) and two surnames (apellidos). The first surname of a person X is the first surname of the father of X, while the second surname of X is the first surname of the mother of X.
In my case,
Saúl - Given name.
Rodríguez - First surname (inherited from my father).
Martín - Second surname (inherited from my mother).

The first surname is considered to be the primary one, while the second surname appears mainly in formal contexts where the full legal name is required, in a role similar to a middle name in English.
So for example, in Spain I could write my name as:
Saúl Rodríguez (Normal)
Saúl Rodríguez Martín (Formal)
Saúl Martín (Never)
