People in Spain usually have a given name (nombre) and two surnames (apellidos):

  • First surname: the first surname of a person X is the first surname of X’s father. This is the main surname and the one normally used in everyday contexts.
  • Second surname: the second surname of X is the first surname of X’s mother. It appears mainly in formal situations where the full legal name is required, in a role similar to a middle name in English.
So I would write my name as:
Saúl Rodríguez (norma)
Saúl Rodríguez Martín (more formal, including second surname)

Citations: I would cite my papers as [Ro], not [Ma].
